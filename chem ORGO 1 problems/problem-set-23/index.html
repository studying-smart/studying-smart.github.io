Problem 1 — Solution (Text)

Classifying each alkene by degree of substitution.

FIRST MOLECULE

Final answer: TRISUBSTITUTED

Solution: Looking at the carbon–carbon double bond, we have a methyl group coming off to the right, and then we have a carbon coming off in front of the double bond, and a carbon coming off from the back of the double bond, so we know it is trisubstituted.

SECOND MOLECULE

Final answer: DISUBSTITUTED

Solution: Looking at the carbon–carbon double bond, we have one carbon coming off the front of the double bond, and one carbon coming off the back of the double bond, so it is disubstituted.

THIRD MOLECULE

Final answer: TRISUBSTITUTED

Solution: Looking at the carbon–carbon double bond, we have a methyl group coming off to the right, and then we have a carbon coming off on each side of the double bond, meaning we have three different carbons coming off it, so it is trisubstituted.

FOURTH MOLECULE

Final answer: TRISUBSTITUTED

Solution: Looking at the carbon–carbon double bond, we have a methyl group coming off to the right, and then two different carbons coming off from the left, so we know it is trisubstituted.

FIFTH MOLECULE

Final answer: MONOSUBSTITUTED

Solution: Here, we only have one carbon coming off from the double bond and since this is the only carbon that is directly bonded to the double bond, we know it is monosubstituted.
